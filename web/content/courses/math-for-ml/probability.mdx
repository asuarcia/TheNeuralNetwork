A model rarely says "this is a cat." It says "85% cat." Under the hood, modern AI is a probability machine, so a working grasp of probability pays off everywhere — from a classifier's confidence to the next-token odds in a chatbot.

## Probability is a number between 0 and 1

A probability measures how likely something is: **0** means impossible, **1** means certain, **0.5** means a coin flip. You can read it as a percentage by multiplying by 100.

The simplest probabilities come from counting: favorable outcomes divided by total outcomes.

## The complement and combining events

Two quick rules cover most everyday reasoning:

- **Complement:** the chance something *doesn't* happen is `1 − P`. If rain is 0.3, no-rain is 0.7.
- **Independent events:** if two things don't affect each other, the chance of *both* is the **product** of their probabilities. Two fair coins both landing heads: `0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25`.

That multiplication rule is the engine behind the Naive Bayes spam filter you'll build in the NLP track.

## Expected value: the long-run average

The **expected value** is what you'd get on average if you repeated something many times: multiply each outcome by its probability and add them up. It's how you reason about risk, rewards, and — in the Reinforcement Learning track — what an action is "worth."

<CodeExercise
  id="prob-ev"
  prompt="A game pays $10 half the time and $20 half the time. Compute the expected payout."
  starter={`values = [10, 20]
probs = [0.5, 0.5]
# Expected value = sum of value * probability
`}
  expected="15.0"
  solution={`values = [10, 20]
probs = [0.5, 0.5]
print(sum(v * p for v, p in zip(values, probs)))`}
/>

<Callout type="tip">
Expected value is the backbone of decision-making under uncertainty. A reinforcement-learning agent picks actions with the highest expected future reward; a trader weighs expected return against risk. Same formula every time.
</Callout>

You can now reason about single uncertain events. The last step is summarizing *whole datasets* of them — the job of statistics.
