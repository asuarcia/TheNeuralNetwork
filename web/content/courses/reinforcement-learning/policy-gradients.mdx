Q-learning learns *values* and picks the highest. **Policy methods** take a different route: learn the **policy** directly — a function that maps a state straight to action probabilities. This family powers modern deep RL and, importantly for this platform, the fine-tuning of language models.

## What is a policy?

A **policy** is the agent's strategy: given a state, it outputs a probability for each action. Good actions get high probability; bad ones get low. To make a set of scores into a valid policy, we normalize them so they sum to 1 (the softmax idea from earlier tracks does exactly this).

The agent then **samples** an action from these probabilities — usually taking the likely one, but keeping some natural exploration built in.

## Policy gradients: nudge toward what worked

Training a policy uses a wonderfully intuitive rule (the **REINFORCE** idea): play out an episode, and if it earned high reward, **increase** the probability of the actions you took; if it earned low reward, **decrease** them. Over many episodes, the policy drifts toward reward-maximizing behavior. It's gradient ascent on expected reward — the same calculus engine as the rest of deep learning, pointed at a new goal.

## Deep RL

Swap the simple table/policy for a **neural network** and you get **deep reinforcement learning** — the approach behind DeepMind's Atari agents, **AlphaGo** (which beat the world Go champion), and robotic control. The network maps high-dimensional states (like raw pixels) to values or action probabilities, trained with the same ideas you just learned.

## RLHF: how chatbots get aligned

Here's why this matters for everything else you've studied. **RLHF — Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback** — is how raw language models become helpful assistants:

1. The model generates several responses.
2. Humans rank which is best, training a **reward model** to imitate those preferences.
3. The language model is then improved with **policy-gradient RL**, where the "reward" is the human-preference score — increasing the probability of responses people prefer.

The chatbot *is* the policy; "be more helpful and harmless" is the reward. The alignment you learned about in Foundations is reinforcement learning under the hood.
