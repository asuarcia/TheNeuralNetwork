People throw the word **AI** at everything from chatbots to dishwashers. So let's be precise.

Artificial intelligence is software that learns patterns from data and uses those patterns to make predictions — instead of following rules a human wrote by hand. That single idea covers spam filters, recommendation feeds, image generators, and the large language models you've been chatting with.

<Callout type="tip">
The shift that made modern AI possible wasn't smarter rules — it was letting the computer **find the rules itself** from huge amounts of examples.
</Callout>

## A model is a prediction machine

Strip away the mystique and a model does one thing: given some input, it predicts a likely output. A language model predicts the next word. An image classifier predicts a label. A recommender predicts what you'll click.

It doesn't "understand" the way you do. It has seen so many examples that its predictions are often uncannily good — and sometimes confidently wrong. Holding both of those facts at once is the core skill of working with AI well.
