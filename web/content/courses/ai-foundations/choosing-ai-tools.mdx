"AI" isn't one product. There's a fast-growing toolbox, and picking the right tool for the job saves you time and disappointment. You don't need any of them to be a programmer — most are just a website or an app you talk to.

## The main categories

- **General chat assistants** — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. Your default for writing, explaining, summarizing, planning, and answering questions. If you're unsure where to start, start here.
- **AI search / research tools** — Perplexity, and the "search" modes inside the big assistants. Best when you need **current** information with links to sources, not just the model's frozen memory.
- **Image generators** — Midjourney, DALL·E, and the image features inside ChatGPT/Gemini. For pictures, logos, illustrations, and mock-ups from a text description.
- **Writing & productivity helpers** — Grammarly, Notion AI, and the AI built into Word, Google Docs, and email. AI that meets you *inside* the app you already use.
- **Voice assistants** — talk-and-listen modes in the major apps. Good hands-free, or for practicing a language.
- **Coding assistants** — GitHub Copilot, Cursor. Only relevant if you write code (the next track gets you started if you're curious).

## Match the task to the tool

A quick mental model — what's the *shape* of what you want back?

- Want **words** (a draft, summary, explanation, plan)? → a chat assistant.
- Want **current facts with sources**? → an AI search tool.
- Want **a picture**? → an image generator.
- Want help **inside a document or inbox**? → the AI built into that app.
- Want to **talk hands-free**? → a voice assistant.

<Callout type="tip">
When a task has several parts, you'll often **chain tools**: ask a chat assistant to draft a newsletter, an image generator to make the header, and your email app's AI to polish the final send. Combining tools is normal — and powerful.
</Callout>

## Free vs. paid — what actually changes

Most tools have a free tier that's genuinely useful. Paying usually gets you:

- Access to the **smarter, newer** model (often a noticeable jump in quality).
- **Higher limits** (more messages, longer documents, image generation).
- **Extra features** — file uploads, web search, voice, image tools.

<Callout type="note">
Start free. Use a tool for real tasks for a week. Only upgrade the *one* you keep hitting limits on — you rarely need to pay for several at once.
</Callout>

## One quiet but important point: your data

Different tools have different policies on what they do with what you type. Before pasting anything sensitive — personal details, work secrets, client info — check whether the tool says it may use your input to train future models, and prefer the ones with clear "we don't train on your data" settings. We'll come back to privacy in the risks lesson.
